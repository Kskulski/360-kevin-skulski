Greatest Innovations

  • One of the greatest innovations in computer science is Margaret Hamilton and her team's code for NASA's launch to the moon in 1969. It was a massive achievement especially considering the ancient technology of the era. There is a famous picture of her next to a 6 foot tall stack of punch cards containing the code.
  • Linux is also a very important innovation in my opinion for computers because it is an open source operating system. This is important as it helps to provide another option for people who do not want to use Windows or Mac and have the know how of building the operating system to match their particularneeds.
    • I saved the best innovation of them all for last. The Internet! While our current version of the internet came much later in the 1990's from a computer scientist named Tim Berners-Lee, the origin of the internet dates back to the 1970's. The first node to node computer network was created in 1969 in fear from the U.S. of an attack on their telecommuncations system from the Soviet Union. One node tried to send the word "Login" to the other node but only the first two letters were received before the network crashed.

Fun Fact!

I was watching a Discovery special about space with my dad and they were discussing the size of the universe and its observable parts. At one point of the episode, they measured the distance from the furthest observable galaxy to the us and found that the lightyears it took for the light to reach Earth is actually greater than the amount of time we estimate the universe to have existed. They go on to explain that this is because the universe is constantly expanding due to an invisible factor to us named dark matter. However, it is not just expanding but stretching. This concept is similar to stretching a rubber between your hands, one hand representing Earth and the other representing the furthest observable galaxy. The light stretches and still reaches us but the distance is expanded. Going back to dark matter for a second, the only way we can observe dark matter is to create a complete vaccuum. Any changes to the material inside (I think it was Helium?) must be caused from an external source which scientists believe is dark matter permeating through the walls of the vaccuum.
